English Pronoun Declension Table

This table summarizes the main forms of English personal pronouns.

Plural we our ours us ourselves

Rule Description Examples (Singular → Plural)
Regular Plurals (Most Nouns) Most nouns form their plural by adding -s.
  • cat → cats
  • book → books
  • table → tables
Nouns ending in -s, -ss, -sh, -ch, -x, -z Add -es to the end of the word.
  • bus → buses
  • class → classes
  • dish → dishes
  • watch → watches
  • box → boxes
  • quiz → quizzes (sometimes doubling the consonant)
Nouns ending in "consonant + y" Change -y to -i and add -es.
  • baby → babies
  • city → cities
  • story → stories
Nouns ending in "vowel + y" Add -s to the end of the word.
  • boy → boys
  • day → days
  • key → keys
Nouns ending in -f, -fe Change -f or -fe to -v and add -es.
(Many exceptions exist)
  • leaf → leaves
  • knife → knives
  • wife → wives
  • wolf → wolves
  • (Exceptions: roof → roofs, chief → chiefs)
Nouns ending in -o Often add -es, but some add only -s.
(Especially for foreign words or shortened forms)
  • potato → potatoes
  • tomato → tomatoes
  • hero → heroes
  • (Exceptions: photo → photos, piano → pianos, radio → radios)
Irregular Plurals These nouns change their form in specific ways.
  • man → men
  • woman → women
  • child → children
  • foot → feet
  • tooth → teeth
  • mouse → mice
  • person → people
Unchanged Plurals (Same Form) The singular and plural forms are identical.
  • sheep → sheep
  • fish → fish (sometimes "fishes" for different species)
  • deer → deer
  • series → series
  • species → species
Latin/Greek Origin Words Some words follow plural rules from their original language.
  • cactus → cacti (or cactuses)
  • fungus → fungi (or funguses)
  • criterion → criteria
  • phenomenon → phenomena
  • datum → data (data is often treated as singular in modern usage)
Tense Usage Positive Form Negative Form Question Form
Present Simple
  • Habits & Routines
  • General Truths & Facts
  • Scheduled Events (future)
 S + Verb (or Verb-s/es for 3rd person singular) S + do/does + not + Verb Do/Does + S + Verb?
Example: He plays tennis every Sunday. / They don't like coffee. / Do you work here?
Present Continuous
  • Actions happening now
  • Temporary actions
  • Developing situations
  • Future plans (fixed arrangements)
 S + am/is/are + Verb-ing S + am/is/are + not + Verb-ing Am/Is/Are + S + Verb-ing?
Example: She is reading a book right now. / They aren't watching TV. / Are you listening?
Present Perfect Simple
  • Actions completed at an unspecified time in the past (result matters now)
  • Actions that started in the past and continue to the present
  • Experiences up to now
 S + have/has + Past Participle S + have/has + not + Past Participle Have/Has + S + Past Participle?
Example: I have finished my homework. / He hasn't eaten yet. / Have you ever been to London?
Present Perfect Continuous
  • Actions that started in the past and are still continuing (focus on duration)
  • Actions that recently stopped (results are visible now)
 S + have/has + been + Verb-ing S + have/has + not + been + Verb-ing Have/Has + S + been + Verb-ing?
Example: She has been studying for hours. / They haven't been waiting long. / Has it been raining?
Past Simple
  • Completed actions in the past at a specific time
  • Series of past actions
  • Past habits (with adverbs)
 S + Verb-ed (or irregular past form) S + did + not + Verb Did + S + Verb?
Example: We visited Paris last year. / I didn't go to the party. / Did you see that movie?
Past Continuous
  • Actions ongoing at a specific time in the past
  • Background action interrupted by another action
  • Parallel actions in the past
 S + was/were + Verb-ing S + was/were + not + Verb-ing Was/Were + S + Verb-ing?
Example: I was reading when you called. / She wasn't sleeping. / Were they working yesterday?
Past Perfect Simple
  • Action completed before another action in the past
 S + had + Past Participle S + had + not + Past Participle Had + S + Past Participle?
Example: She had finished her work before he arrived. / I hadn't seen him before then. / Had you eaten anything?
Past Perfect Continuous
  • Action that continued up to a certain point in the past (focus on duration)
 S + had + been + Verb-ing S + had + not + been + Verb-ing Had + S + been + Verb-ing?
Example: He had been waiting for an hour when she finally came. / We hadn't been trying for long. / Had it been snowing all night?
Future Simple (Will)
  • Predictions
  • Spontaneous decisions
  • Promises, offers, threats
 S + will + Verb S + will + not (won't) + Verb Will + S + Verb?
Example: I will call you later. / It won't rain tomorrow. / Will you help me?
Future Simple (Be Going To)
  • Plans & Intentions
  • Predictions based on present evidence
 S + am/is/are + going to + Verb S + am/is/are + not + going to + Verb Am/Is/Are + S + going to + Verb?
Example: We are going to visit Japan next year. / She isn't going to buy that car. / Are you going to study?
Future Continuous
  • Actions ongoing at a specific time in the future
  • Future events that are part of a routine
 S + will + be + Verb-ing S + will + not (won't) + be + Verb-ing Will + S + be + Verb-ing?
Example: At 10 AM tomorrow, I will be working. / They won't be sleeping when you arrive. / Will you be waiting for me?
Future Perfect Simple
  • Action that will be completed before a certain time in the future
 S + will + have + Past Participle S + will + not (won't) + have + Past Participle Will + S + have + Past Participle?
Example: By next year, I will have graduated from university. / She won't have finished by then. / Will you have learned English?
Future Perfect Continuous
  • Action that will have continued up to a certain point in the future (focus on duration)
S + will + have + been + Verb-ing S + will + not (won't) + have + been + Verb-ing Will + S + have + been + Verb-ing?
Example: By 5 PM, I will have been working for 8 hours. / They won't have been living here for long. / Will you have been studying for a while?
